## Gross margin at current pricing

| Plan | Price/mo | COGS upper bound (heavy user) | Gross margin |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Free | $0 | $0.56 | -∞ (acquisition cost) |
| **Pro** | $19 | $3.76 | **80.2%** |
| Team (5 seats) | $59 | $18.80 (5× heavy) | **68.1%** |

A 100× abuser costs us ~$380/mo on Pro. We rate-limit before that. See below.

## Rate-limit ceilings (`lib/plan-limits.ts` → `RECALL_LIMITS`)

| Plan | Soft (advisory) | Hard (429) |
|---|---:|---:|
| Free | 50/day | 50/day |
| Pro | 1,000/day | 5,000/day |
| Team | 50,000/day | unlimited |

**Math at the hard cap, daily** (assumes the user hits the cap every
day for 30 days, every recall does a Haiku rerank on a worst-case
pool):

- Free at 50 recalls × 30 days × $0.0013 per recall = **$1.95/mo COGS**.
  Pure acquisition spend. Acceptable.
- Pro at 5,000 recalls × 30 days × $0.0013 per recall = **$195/mo COGS**.
  At $19/mo, this is a 10× loss. **A pathological user at the hard
  ceiling burns ten months of subscription per day.**
- Team has no hard ceiling but soft-throttles at 50k/day — at that
  level we'd get a Slack alert and have a human conversation.

The hard caps are calibrated to cover **realistic power use** (Roman
hammering Spine on a 14-hour build day = 200-400 recalls). Anything
above that is a script. 5000 catches it before it bankrupts the unit
economics.

## Scaling assumptions

| Users | Mostly Free | Mostly Pro | Server load | Monthly COGS |
|---:|---|---|---|---:|
| 1k | 80% / 20% | mix | < 1% of one box | ~$80 |
| 10k | 70% / 25% / 5% Team | mix | One box, comfortably | ~$800 |
| 100k | 60% / 30% / 10% Team | mix | 3-5 boxes + read replica | ~$8,000 |

The COGS column is variable only. Fixed infra at 100k users — call it
$2k/mo of multi-region Postgres + read replicas + Coolify cluster — gives
a total COGS ~$10k against revenue of ~$300k (if 30% of 100k convert at
average $19) for a **~97% gross margin business at 100k users.**

## Sensitivity analysis

These numbers move in the WRONG direction if:

1. **Haiku output bloat.** We currently spec 5-10 picks. If the model
   verboses the rerank rationale, output cost can 5×. Mitigation:
   structured-output mode + max_tokens cap.
2. **Embedding model upgrade.** text-embedding-3-large is 5× more
   expensive at $0.13/1M. Even at 5×, it's $0.0002/session — still
   negligible. Safe to upgrade if quality demands it.
3. **Failed reranks retried.** A 2× retry budget on Haiku failures
   doubles per-recall cost. Mitigation: bounded retry (2 attempts, then
   fall back to fused-only).
4. **Power-user concentration.** If 5% of users do 90% of recalls, we
   need to size hard caps tighter. Re-run this audit with skewed
   profile weights to model that.

## What this gates

This page is the answer to the implicit "how do you know charging $19
isn't a money loser?" question. With ~80% gross margins on Pro at the
heavy-user ceiling and hard-cap protection against pathological use,
the answer is "the math works." If any line above changes — vendor
pricing, rate limits, session profile — re-run
`scripts/token-audit.ts` and update both the audit and this file.
